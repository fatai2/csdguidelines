COVID-19: Quarantine for staff and visitors to the Unit

Executive summary

Introduction

The Gambia has transited through several phases of the COVID-19 pandemic and is currently experiencing the community transmission phase. COVID-19 is caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, that spreads from person-to-person through droplet and contact transmission. To control the spread of COVID-19, interventions need to break the chains of human-to-human transmission, and ensure that the number of new cases generated by each confirmed case remains below 1 (R0 < 1). Quarantine is an age-long public health strategy that can help in preventing the introduction of diseases to new areas or reduce human-to-human transmission of SARs-CoV-2 virus in areas where it is circulating.

MRCG@LSHTM, by virtue of its leading role in research in West-Africa is a host to several travellers from other countries and also it has a large body of staff in several research projects which interact with members of the community and among themselves. It is therefore pertinent to draft a guideline which enable the effective implementation of quarantine, based on the best available evidence, and responsive to our needs as a unit during the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic. The guideline should be implemented bearing in mind the Government’s COVID-19 quarantine guidelines.

Definition of Quarantine

The quarantine of persons is the restriction of activities or the separation of persons who are not ill but who may have been exposed to COVID-19, with the objective of monitoring their symptoms and ensuring the early detection and isolation of cases.

Implementation

Category 1: Travellers coming into the MRCG@LSHTM.

There will no longer be any quarantine beyond what the Gambian government requires for incoming travellers (currently, a negative test prior to travel). Travellers should alert SCRIC prior to their arrival. They will be tested on arrival, day 3 and on day 7. If any of these tests should be positive, they will be isolated as is standard practice if they test positive.

Category 2: Staff who have significant contact with a confirmed COVID-19 case

“Significant contact” is defined as being within one metre of a confirmed COVID-19 case for 15 minutes and above without the use of a facemask by the case, and the contact.

Travelling in proximity with (that is, within 1 m separation from) a COVID-19 patient in any kind of conveyance.

This category is further subdivided into two (2):

Category 2a: Staff of the CSD/laboratory department who had significant contact with a confirmed COVID-19 case.

This category of staff is expected to keep working while abiding by the unit’s advisory regarding the use of PPEs, regular hand hygiene, disinfection of regular touch surfaces and maintaining physical distancing. They are to undergo a SARs-CoV-2 PCR test between day 5 and day 7. If positive they go on to self-isolate, but if negative, they continue working.

Category 2b: Staff in other departments who had significant contact with a Confirmed COVID-19 case

This category of staff are to self-quarantine at home if their house is conducive, and they are to undergo the SARs-CoV-2 PCR test on day 7. If Positive they go on to self-isolate, but if negative, they continue working.

Category 3: MRCG@LSHTM drivers who by virtue of official duties visit neighbouring countries

This category of staff will present a negative SARS-CoV-2 PCR test conducted within 72 hours of travelling in order to be allowed to cross into Senegal and again on their return to The Gambia. In recognition of the similar risk on both sides of the border, they will not need to quarantine.

Steps to take during Quarantine

  • Stay / work at home, preferably in a separate room and limit contact with those in the same house and/or compound.

  • Monitor your health i.e. watch out for symptoms like fever, cough, difficulty in breathing, sore throat.

  • Ensure you communicate regularly with the health officer assigned to monitor the quarantine process.

  • Regular hand washing with soap and water for at least 20 secs or use of alcohol hand-rub

  • Limit the use of shared spaces like kitchen, bathroom, toilet amongst others.
